"Centre of the heart and community"

What is Naawi-Oodena?

Naawi-Oodena, meaning "centre of the heart and community" in Anishinaabemowin, is Canada's largest urban reserve, located in southwest Winnipeg. Spanning approximately 160 acres, it is jointly managed by all seven Treaty 1 First Nations and the Canada Lands Company. Formerly the Kapyong Barracks, this site has been transformed into a thriving community that blends Indigenous culture with modern urban living. Naawi-Oodenan aims to generate economic investments and connect communities together.

Lower Density Areas

At Naawi-Oodena, lower density areas focus on creating quiet, spacious neighborhoods. These parts of the community have single-family homes with yards and plenty of green space, giving families more privacy while staying connected to nature and culture.

Medium Density Areas

Medium density areas in Naawi-Oodena include townhouses, duplexes, and low-rise apartments. These homes are built closer together than in low-density areas but still offer some private space. They are designed to support more people while keeping a strong sense of community and easy access to parks, shops, and gathering places.

Community Spaces

Naawi-Oodena includes many thoughtful community spaces that bring people together and celebrate Indigenous culture. These spaces include parks, walking trails, gathering areas, and playgrounds where families and children can enjoy the outdoors. There are also green spaces for ceremonies, events, and everyday relaxation. These open areas help create a strong sense of community and allow people to connect with nature and each other in a peaceful, welcoming environment. In addition to outdoor spaces, Naawi-Oodena will feature cultural centers and buildings where people can learn, share stories, and take part in workshops or celebrations. These spaces are designed by and for Indigenous people, making sure they reflect traditional knowledge and values. By blending natural spaces with cultural gathering places, Naawi-Oodena supports both community life and cultural identity in a modern, urban setting.
